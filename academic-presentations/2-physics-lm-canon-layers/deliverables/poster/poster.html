Physics of Language Models: Part 4 — Architecture Design and the Magic of Canon Layers

Author: Zeyuan Allen-Zhu (FAIR at Meta)

Conference: NeurIPS 2025 (Part 4.1) + Code Release (Part 4.2)

Motivation

Architecture comparison at academic scale is noisy enough to hide real signal. At 1.3B parameters and 100B tokens, random seeds alone can move benchmark accuracy by 2-4 points, often the same size as the reported gain from a new architecture. Pretraining loss is also a weak proxy for reasoning: several linear-state models optimize perplexity well yet underperform on multi-hop tasks. Finally, data quality confounds comparisons because weak corpora suppress capability emergence and blur architectural differences.

  • Challenge 1: loss quality does not equal capability quality.
  • Challenge 2: seed noise dominates architecture signal.
  • Challenge 3: data and optimization confounds mask causality.

Physics analogy: isolate atomic variables first, then test in reality.

Background

The paper evaluates four architecture families under a shared synthetic setup: Transformer, GLA, Mamba2, and GDN. Canon is introduced as a horizontal residual primitive that can be inserted across families.

Part 4.1 reproduces known qualitative rankings from real-world pretraining but in a cleaner regime: Transformers and GDN generally lead reasoning depth, Mamba2 and GLA favor efficient memory-style behavior, and Canon shifts this frontier by adding explicit local transport between adjacent token states.

  • Transformer: strong reasoning depth, expensive global mixing.
  • GLA/Mamba2/GDN: efficient long-context memory via linear-state updates.
  • Canon: shared local mixing primitive across both families.

Synthetic Playground

Five pretraining tasks decompose language intelligence into atomic skills: Depo (depth), Brevo (breadth), Capo (capacity), Mano (manipulation), and Lano (hierarchical structure). This removes real-data confounds and enables low-cost architecture science.

  • Depo: multi-hop graph reasoning with controllable hop count.
  • Brevo: DAG topological ordering with high branching.
  • Capo: biological knowledge extraction from synthetic genomes.
  • Mano: arithmetic chain manipulation and composition.
  • Lano: context-free grammar understanding and generation.

Key Takeaway

Synthetic tasks isolate atomic skills, enabling clean architectural comparisons at low cost.
  • Low compute budget, high measurement fidelity.
  • Controlled depth and breadth reveal causal design effects.
  • Findings transfer to trillion-token pretraining.

Canon Layers

Canon layers inject local token-to-token mixing through horizontal residual links. The same mechanism can be inserted at four Transformer integration points (A/B/C/D) and adapted to linear-state models. The paper's musical analogy is direct: motifs repeat with short delays, and hidden states similarly benefit from short-range delayed reuse.

  • Canon-A/C: before attention and before MLP normalization paths.
  • Canon-B/D: inside attention and MLP channels for richer mixing.
$$h'_t = h_t + \text{causal\_conv1d}_w([h_t, h_{t-1}, h_{t-2}, h_{t-3}])$$

Canon-ABCD adds less than 0.5% parameters but unlocks major capability gains.

Key Insight

Canon layers add horizontal residual links, like residual connections but across neighboring tokens. Even random-weight Canon notably enhances performance.

The pathway matters first; learned weights refine it later.

Part 4.1 Results

In synthetic pretraining, Transformer + Canon reaches 2-4x deeper reasoning and about 30% better breadth. For linear models, Canon strongly upgrades GLA and closes much of the gap to Mamba2 and GDN.

  • RoPE + Canon learns deeper multi-hop reasoning trajectories.
  • NoPE + Canon revives position-free Transformers.
  • GLA + Canon can match or exceed stronger linear baselines.

Scaling to Reality [4.2]

Part 4.2 validates synthetic predictions at industrial scale. The release contains 16 Llama/LlamaCanon models and 48 linear models (GLA, GDN, Mamba2 variants) at 1B, 3B, and 8B parameters trained on up to 2T tokens. Across all 8 controlled Transformer settings, LlamaCanon beats Llama by roughly 2 MMLU points on average. For linear families, GLA + Canon reaches GDN-level performance and frequently exceeds Mamba2.

The scaling study also reports compute-normalized tradeoffs: LlamaCanon tends to move upward on the quality frontier without requiring a larger model class. In linear-model sweeps, Canon improves average lm_eval and long-context retrieval behavior most strongly for GLA, moderately for GDN, and least for Mamba2, mirroring the synthetic prediction that Mamba2 already contains partial Canon-like mixing through conv1d-style pathways.

Error Analysis

The limiting factor for linear-state models is not raw memory size but memory dynamics: repeated compression and retrieval introduce compounding error. This appears in short-context retrieval and especially in 2-hop reasoning, where models still fail even at 8B and 1T tokens. Canon helps but does not fully remove this bottleneck.

Intuitively, each step can preserve most of the needed signal, but multi-step chains multiply small distortions. This is why 1-hop probes may look acceptable while 2-hop and deeper tasks collapse. The paper argues this is a dynamics problem rather than a capacity ceiling, and motivates hybrid stacks where selective attention handles deeper reasoning while linear-state modules provide efficient long-context processing.

2-hop failure persists at scale: the bottleneck is error propagation, not parameter count.

Conclusion & Future

Canon is a lightweight architectural primitive that improves Transformers and linear models alike. Future work: dynamic Canon kernels, hybrid attention-linear stacks, and richer synthetic probes for memory dynamics.

The broader claim is methodological: low-noise synthetic pretraining can act as a fast architecture laboratory, then large-scale training can validate transfer. This pipeline reduces wasted compute and improves confidence in architecture decisions before expensive deployment-scale runs.

  • Canonical local mixing should be treated as a default design component.
  • Hybrid architectures can combine deep reasoning and long-context efficiency.
  • Mechanistic diagnostics should track retrieval error accumulation over hops.
