Prior Work: Formal Languages Meet Neural Networks

Several lines of work have explored what neural networks learn about language structure. Each provides important insights, but none offers a complete mechanistic picture.

Work Approach Finding Limitation
Deletang et al. 2023 Tested NNs on Chomsky hierarchy Transformers struggle with context-free tasks Very short sequences (length ≤10); toy-scale models
Hewitt & Manning 2019 Structural probes on BERT Parse tree distances encoded in embeddings Probing only — no mechanism for how structure is computed
Olsson et al. 2022 Mechanistic interp. of induction heads In-context copying circuits Simple pattern matching; not hierarchical composition
Zhao et al. 2023 Transformers parse while predicting Concurrent finding: implicit parsing Theoretical focus; limited empirical depth
Murty et al. 2023 Intrinsic compositionality Tree structure in representations Real language only; no controlled experiments
The Gap: No prior work provides a controlled study showing that transformers learn hierarchical parsing at realistic scale, with both probing evidence (what is learned) and mechanistic evidence (how it is computed via attention).
